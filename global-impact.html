A Community Recognized Worldwide

For over five decades, the African Hebrew Israelites of Jerusalem have transformed barren desert land into a thriving oasis, pioneered sustainable living practices, and made significant contributions to health, culture, and peace initiatives globally.

Our community's journey from uncertain status to official recognition represents a testament to resilience, vision, and peaceful coexistence.

Our Vision

To share sustainable living practices and preventative health approaches that benefit humanity while preserving our unique cultural heritage.

Our Mission

To build bridges between cultures, develop regenerative environmental solutions, and advance health practices that enhance quality of life worldwide.

Our Journey to Recognition

1969

First members of the community arrive in Israel, beginning the journey toward establishing the Village of Peace in Dimona.

1990-1991

The Israeli government grants B-1 work visas to community members, marking the first official recognition after two decades.

2003

The Ministry of Interior confers permanent residency status on community members, a milestone achievement documented by international media.

2004

The Israel Defense Forces formally welcomes community members, a significant step in their integration into Israeli society.

2008

President Shimon Peres visits the Village of Peace, stating: "Your community is beloved in Israel. You give the country happiness and song and hope for a better world."

2009

Elyakim Ben-Israel becomes the first member of the community to receive Israeli citizenship without formal conversion to Judaism.

2012

Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's office sends official greetings during New World Passover celebrations, further validating the community's place in Israeli society.

2016

The UNDP highlights the African Hebrew Development Agency's desert agriculture techniques as a sustainable development model in arid regions.

African Government Collaborations

Ghana Ministry of Health:

Collaboration to establish the Regenerative Health and Nutrition program when Major (rtd) Courage Quashigah was Minister of Health, documented in the Ghana Health Service Annual Report (2010).

South African Engagement:

A delegation from South Africa's KwaZulu-Natal province visited the Village of Peace in 2011 to study their sustainable community model for potential implementation in rural areas, according to the South African Department of International Relations and Cooperation.

Academic & Research Recognition

Ben-Gurion University Collaboration:

Joint conference on 'Alternative Health and Communal Well-being' in 2013, bringing together scholars and community health practitioners.

Harvard School of Public Health:

Researchers documented interest in the community's nutritional outcomes, noting that "the community's multigenerational adherence to a strict plant-based diet presents a unique opportunity to study long-term health outcomes of vegan nutrition in a controlled environment" (Harvard Public Health Review, 2017).

Cultural Achievements

Eurovision Representation

Eddie Butler, a member of the community, represented Israel at the Eurovision Song Contest in 1999 and 2006, bringing international attention to the cultural contributions of African Hebrew Israelites.

Music & Performance

Museum Exhibitions

The Israel Museum featured the community's cultural contributions in the exhibition 'Threads of Identity,' including traditional garments and textile arts, recognizing their contribution to Israel's diverse cultural landscape.

Visual Arts

Culinary Innovation

"Long before Tel Aviv became known as a vegan capital, the African Hebrew Israelites were pioneering plant-based cuisine in Israel" (Israel21c, 2018). The community's innovative approach to vegan cooking has influenced Israel's food culture.

Culinary Arts

Cultural Tourism

The Village of Peace has become a unique cultural tourism destination, attracting thousands of visitors annually interested in sustainable living, vegan cuisine, and the community's distinctive music and art (Israel Ministry of Tourism, 2019).

Health & Environmental Innovations

Preventative Health Model

The community's plant-based nutrition approach has been studied for its effectiveness in preventing chronic diseases. Medical research published in the Israeli Medical Association Journal (2016) showed significantly lower rates of hypertension and diabetes among community members.

"The preventative health practices of the Dimona community, particularly their dietary approach and regular physical activity, demonstrate principles that could be applied more broadly in chronic disease prevention programs."
- American Journal of Public Health, 2018

The WHO Regional Office for Africa has referenced these practices in technical reports on alternative nutritional approaches for chronic disease prevention.

Environmental Sustainability

The community's desert agriculture techniques have transformed arid land into productive gardens, creating a model of sustainability in challenging environments. Their water recycling system received recognition in the 2016 Green Innovation Awards for reducing water consumption by up to 60%.

  • Certified as organic growers by the Israel Bio-Organic Agricultural Association (2015)
  • Developed innovative drip irrigation systems for desert agriculture
  • Created permaculture systems that maintain soil health in arid conditions

The UNDP has highlighted these practices as a sustainable development model applicable to arid regions worldwide.

Humanitarian Contributions

African Development Projects

Our community's international development work extends across several African nations, bringing sustainable solutions to communities in need.

Clean Water Initiative

The African Hebrew Development Agency's clean water projects have installed eight water wells in Ghana's Volta Region, providing reliable access to clean drinking water for communities previously relying on contaminated sources.

Educational Support

Our community sponsors educational programs in rural areas, providing materials, teacher training, and infrastructure support to improve access to quality education.

Peace Advocacy

Creating bridges between cultures and promoting peaceful coexistence is a cornerstone of our mission.

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr./SCLC Institute

Established by our community, the institute has been recognized for its conflict resolution training programs implemented in schools throughout southern Israel, as documented by the Peres Center for Peace.
