Feast of Tabernacles (Sukkhote)

[Image Placeholder: Sukkah or community celebrating Sukkhote]

The **Feast of Tabernacles (Sukkhote)** is a joyous, week-long festival commemorating the period when the ancient Israelites dwelled in temporary shelters (sukkot) during their forty years in the wilderness after the exodus from Egypt. It is a time of thanksgiving for Yah's provision and protection.

For the African Hebrew Israelites, Sukkhote holds special resonance, connecting their own **wilderness experience** in Liberia to the historical journey of their ancestors. The community observes this feast with vibrant **community celebrations**, often involving the construction of temporary dwellings (sukkot), special meals, music, dance, and gatherings.

A significant aspect of the celebration is the **pilgrimage to Jerusalem**, where community members gather for worship and reflection, reinforcing their connection to the Holy City and the fulfillment of prophecy in their return to the land. Sukkhote serves as a powerful reminder of Yah's faithfulness throughout history and in the community's own modern-day journey.
