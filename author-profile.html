Prince Gavriel Ha Gadol: Author of "The Impregnable People" and Founding Pioneer

The Living Witness to Prophecy Fulfilled

[Image Placeholder: Prince Gavriel Ha Gadol]

Prince Gavriel Ha Gadol stands as one of the foundational pillars of the African Hebrew Israelite community in Dimona, Israel. As the author of "The Impregnable People," the comprehensive chronicle of the community's prophetic journey, and as one of the first to set foot in the Holy Land in 1969, Prince Gavriel embodies the living fulfillment of biblical prophecy concerning the return of the scattered children of Israel to their ancestral homeland.

Early Life and Divine Preparation

Born George Butler on February 28, 1937, in Delhi, Louisiana during the latter years of the Great Depression, Prince Gavriel experienced firsthand the harsh realities of segregation in the American South. His family joined the Great Migration northward in 1942, settling in Chicago where young George received his primary education at Jenner Elementary School and later attended Washburn Trade School.

Following his graduation in 1955, he worked at Howard Foundry as an apprentice core and mold-maker—the same foundry where his father and grandfather had worked. He later became a bus driver for the Chicago Transit Authority, which he considered a respectable position for a Black man during that era. In 1958, he married his first wife (later known as Karaliyah), and they established a family, eventually having sons Kashoov, Avraham, and Gavriel.

Despite achieving what would have been considered success by the standards of his time—a stable job, a home, and a family—Prince Gavriel remained spiritually unfulfilled. Like many African Americans in the 1960s, he sensed that something essential was missing from his identity and purpose.

Spiritual Awakening and the Exodus

The pivotal moment in Prince Gavriel's life came in the mid-1960s when, through his friendship with Ben Ammi (formerly Ben Carter), he discovered his true identity as a Hebrew Israelite. This revelation transformed his understanding of himself, his people, and their history. As documented by anthropologist Fran Markowitz, this awakening was not merely adopting a new religion but reclaiming an ancestral identity that had been obscured through centuries of captivity and cultural displacement.

When Ben Ammi received the divine vision from the Angel Gabriel in 1966 instructing him to lead the people back to the Promised Land, Prince Gavriel became one of his most trusted supporters. In 1967, when the first group of approximately 400 Hebrew Israelites departed from America for Liberia, Prince Gavriel was among them, helping to organize and lead this modern exodus.

The Wilderness Period and Purification

During the challenging years in Liberia (1967-1969), Prince Gavriel demonstrated remarkable resilience and leadership. As one of the early scouts who traveled to Liberia to prepare the way, he played a crucial role in purchasing land and establishing the infrastructure for the community's settlement.

In interviews documented by researchers like John L. Jackson Jr., Prince Gavriel has described the intense hardships of the Liberian wilderness: venomous snakes, driver ants, torrential monsoon rains, tropical diseases, and the difficult transition from urban life to subsistence agriculture. Many who began the journey turned back, unable to endure these trials, but Prince Gavriel remained steadfast. As he later wrote in "The Impregnable People," these challenges served as a divine crucible, burning away the "mentality of Babylon" and preparing the Saints for their ultimate destination in the Holy Land.

Pioneer of the Return to the Promised Land

On August 19, 1969, Prince Gavriel made history as part of the first family to immigrate from Liberia to Israel, along with Emah Tekia and their son Benainu. This arrival marked the beginning of the prophetic return that would bring thousands of Hebrew Israelites back to the land of their ancestors after nearly 2,000 years of exile.

The early years in Israel brought new challenges—limited housing, economic struggles, legal uncertainties, and cultural adjustment. Prince Gavriel endured multiple deportations, separations from family, and the constant pressure of building a new society in an often unwelcoming environment. Yet throughout these trials, his commitment to establishing Yah's Kingdom never wavered.

Leadership in the Divine Council

In 1971, when the community gathered at what became known as Har Kayeem (Mountain of Life) in Dimona, Prince Gavriel was appointed by Ben Ammi as one of the twelve Princes to form the Divine Council. He received the spiritual title "Prince of Love," reflecting his compassionate nature and his role in fostering community harmony.

As a member of the Divine Council, Prince Gavriel has contributed to governing the community according to principles of righteousness, transparency, and spiritual insight. He has been instrumental in:

  • Introducing fenugreek (known as "Love Powder") as a healing herb
  • Helping establish the Divine Marriage system
  • Contributing to the development of Genesis Nutrition principles
  • Guiding the community through periods of external opposition
  • Providing spiritual counsel and teaching

Author of "The Impregnable People"

Prince Gavriel's most enduring contribution may be his authorship of "The Impregnable People," the definitive chronicle of the African Hebrew Israelites' journey. This two-volume work documents in detail the exodus from America, the wilderness experience in Liberia, and the establishment of the Kingdom in Israel.

As noted by scholars who have studied the community, Prince Gavriel's writing combines personal testimony with historical and spiritual reflection, offering readers both an intimate and panoramic view of this remarkable exodus. His narrative style weaves together:

  • Authentic emotional accounts of both challenges and triumphs
  • Spiritual interpretation of events through a Hebrew Israelite perspective
  • Vivid descriptions of life in America, Liberia, and Israel
  • Thoughtful examination of Western society and its impact on Black Americans
  • Unwavering conviction in the divine purpose behind their journey

The title "The Impregnable People" itself speaks to the community's unshakeable spirit that has withstood tremendous opposition. Like the kingdom prophesied by Daniel that "shall never be destroyed," the African Hebrew Israelites have demonstrated a spiritual fortitude that no earthly power could break.

Health Pioneer and Advocate

Beyond his leadership and literary contributions, Prince Gavriel has been a pioneer in the community's revolutionary approach to health and wellness. Researchers from Ben-Gurion University and other institutions have documented how he helped introduce and popularize fenugreek as a medicinal herb in Israel, predating scientific validation of its benefits for diabetes management, hormone balance, and digestive health.

As noted in the Journal of Ethnopharmacology (2019), Prince Gavriel's advocacy for this herb—which he named "Love Powder"—contributed to its adoption in natural health circles beyond the community. His intuitive understanding of plant medicine reflects the community's broader approach to health that integrates spiritual wisdom with practical application.

Prince Gavriel has also been a vocal advocate for the Genesis Diet (complete plant-based nutrition) since its community-wide adoption in 1971. His testimonies about the transformative health benefits of this approach have been cited in studies of the community's exceptional health outcomes, including their remarkably low rates of hypertension, diabetes, and heart disease compared to control populations.

Cultural Ambassador and Bridge Builder

As one of the longest-serving members of the community, Prince Gavriel has represented the African Hebrew Israelites in various international forums. He has helped build relationships with African nations, including Ghana where the community established health initiatives in collaboration with the Ministry of Health.

Israeli media has documented his role in hosting visitors to the Village of Peace, from curious tourists to official delegations. His ability to articulate the community's journey and vision has helped transform public perception from initial skepticism to growing respect.

In 2008, he was present when Israeli President Shimon Peres visited the Village of Peace and declared, "Your community is beloved in Israel. You give the country happiness and song and hope for a better world." This moment represented a significant shift in official recognition of the community's positive contributions to Israeli society.

Living Legacy and Continuing Influence

Today, Prince Gavriel continues to serve as an elder statesman of the community in the Village of Peace. His wisdom guides younger generations who know him as a living link to their founding history. As anthropologist John L. Jackson Jr. has observed, elders like Prince Gavriel provide continuity and perspective that help the community navigate the challenges of maintaining their unique identity while engaging with the modern world.

Prince Gavriel has lived to see remarkable transformations—from the community's uncertain beginnings to their current status as permanent residents and citizens of Israel, from their early struggles with acceptance to their recognized contributions to Israeli culture, from their pioneering of plant-based living to Israel's emergence as a global leader in veganism.

"When I look at our community today—the Village of Peace, our healthy children and grandchildren, the international recognition we've received—I see not just our accomplishment but divine vindication. We were ex-Negroes from America's ghettos—people systematically stripped of identity, dignity, and strength. Now we stand as Hebrew Israelites firmly established in the Holy Land, unbroken despite decades of opposition. This transformation is the true miracle."

His life and writings stand as testimony that Yah's word never returns void—that the promises made to the children of Israel have found their fulfillment in our time.

Literary Works

  • The Impregnable People, Part One: Exodus from Captivity (1994) - Chronicles the awakening from spiritual death, the divine vision of 1966, and the exodus through the wilderness of Liberia
  • The Impregnable People, Part Two: Kingdom Established (2002) - Documents the arrival in the Holy Land, the formation of the Divine Council, and the twenty-year period of testing and establishment in Israel
  • Contributions to community publications including the Jerusalem Chronicle and Soul Messenger

Recognition and Honors

  • Appointed to the Divine Council as Prince of Love (1971)
  • Featured in academic studies by researchers including John L. Jackson Jr., Fran Markowitz, and Merrill Singer
  • Recognized for contributions to the community's health paradigm in a 2016 Ben-Gurion University study on preventative health
  • Honored at the community's 50th anniversary in Israel (2019)
  • Included in the Israel Museum's "Threads of Identity" exhibition documenting diverse cultural communities in Israel (2018)

Prince Gavriel Ha Gadol's life embodies the journey of the African Hebrew Israelites—from spiritual death to resurrection, from captivity to liberty, from loss of identity to the reclamation of divine purpose. His written accounts preserve this remarkable story for future generations, ensuring that the prophetic return of the children of Israel to their homeland will be remembered as one of the most extraordinary spiritual pilgrimages of modern times.
