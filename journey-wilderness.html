Wilderness Purification (1967–1969)

[Image Placeholder: Liberian landscape or community during purification]

The journey from America led the pioneers not directly to Israel, but to the West African nation of Liberia. This period, lasting approximately three and a half years, served as a crucial phase of wilderness purification, mirroring the experiences of ancient Israel after their exodus from Egypt.

Life in the Liberian interior presented immense challenges. The community faced unfamiliar natural elements, including venomous snakes, driver ants, and torrential monsoon rains. Resources were scarce, and the transition from urban American life to subsistence living in a tropical environment tested their physical and spiritual endurance.

"We chose to stay there... in order to get rid of the hate that was in us at that time, to get rid of our bitterness… to learn to be righteous."
— Rakhimim (Community Member, quoted by Fran Markowitz)

This period was essential for shedding the "mentality of Babylon"—the attitudes, habits, and spiritual compromises acquired during centuries of captivity. It was a time of intense spiritual refining, community building, and learning to rely wholly on divine guidance and provision. Many who began the journey could not withstand the purification process and returned to America, but those who persevered emerged stronger, unified, and prepared for the next stage: the return to the Promised Land.
