Feast of Unleavened Bread

[Image Placeholder: Unleavened bread or related imagery]

Immediately following Passover, the community observes the **Feast of Unleavened Bread**. This seven-day period commemorates the haste with which the ancient Israelites left Egypt, having no time for their bread to rise (Exodus 12:39).

During this time, the Saints abstain from consuming any leavened products, symbolizing the removal of sin and corruption (represented by leaven) from their lives and homes. It is a period of purification and rededication to living according to divine law.

The Feast involves special gatherings, prayers, and the consumption of unleavened bread (matzah) prepared according to community traditions and Genesis Nutrition principles. It serves as a reminder of the need for spiritual purity and the community's ongoing journey away from the "leaven" of worldly influences.
