Kingdom Celebrations

[Image Placeholder: Community celebrating one of the Kingdom Celebrations]

In addition to the traditional biblical Holy Days, the African Hebrew Israelite community observes several unique **Kingdom Celebrations** that commemorate significant events and principles central to their modern experience and identity.

New World Passover

This celebration specifically commemorates the community's exodus from America in 1967. It connects the ancient Passover story of liberation from Egypt with their own modern deliverance from spiritual and physical captivity in the West, highlighting the continuity of Yah's redemptive plan for His people.

Day of Appreciation (Yom L'Mokareem)

Yom L'Mokareem is a day set aside to express gratitude and appreciation for the elders, leaders (like H.E. Ben Ammi and the Princes), and pioneers who sacrificed and persevered to establish the Kingdom. It fosters respect for leadership and acknowledges the collective effort involved in building the community.

National Vegan Day

Observed weekly on Wednesdays, this celebration underscores the community's deep commitment to Genesis Nutrition and the plant-based lifestyle adopted in 1971. It serves as a regular affirmation of their dietary principles and the health benefits they have experienced as pioneers of veganism.

These Kingdom Celebrations, alongside the traditional Holy Days, create a rich tapestry of remembrance, thanksgiving, and rededication throughout the year, reinforcing the community's unique history, values, and divine mission.
