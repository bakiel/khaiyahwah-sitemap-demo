Divine Governance: The Divine Council

[Image Placeholder: The Divine Council or symbolic representation of righteous governance]

On October 24, 1971, at Har Kayeem (Mountain of Life) in Dimona, H.E. Ben Ammi established the Divine Council, marking the foundation of righteous governance in the Holy Land after nearly 2,000 years. This structure serves as a divine alternative to the corrupted political systems of the world.

Following the biblical pattern shown by Moses (Exodus 18), the Divine Council comprises twelve Princes, each appointed with a spiritual title reflecting their specific role (e.g., Prince of Mercy, Prince of Crafts, Prince of Wisdom, Prince of Love). H.E. Ben Ammi served as the thirteenth Prince, providing overall spiritual leadership and guidance.

Unlike worldly governments, the Divine Council operates based on principles of righteousness, transparency, and spiritual insight. Its functions include:

  • •Interpreting and applying divine law for the community.
  • •Rendering judgments in disputes and matters of conduct.
  • •Overseeing community institutions (Kingdom School, House of Life, economic enterprises).
  • •Guiding spiritual development and instruction.
  • •Representing the community in external relations.

The Divine Council continues to function as the spiritual leadership of the African Hebrew Israelite community, fulfilling the prophecy of Isaiah 1:26:

"And I will restore thy judges as at the first, and thy counsellors as at the beginning: afterward thou shalt be called, The city of righteousness, the faithful city."

This system of Divine Governance ensures that the community remains aligned with Yah's will, fostering order, justice, and spiritual progress within the Kingdom.
