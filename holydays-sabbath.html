The Sabbath (Shabbat)

"Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy." — Exodus 20:8
[Image Placeholder: Community observing Sabbath or symbolic imagery]

The Sabbath (Shabbat) is the most honoured of the High Holy Days within the Kingdom of Yah—a weekly appointment set aside for **spiritual communion** with the Creator through fasting, meditation, and sanctification. Observed from sunset Friday to sunset Saturday, the Saints abstain from physical nourishment, drawing only on spiritual sustenance during this sacred time.

During the Sabbath:

  • No food, water, or work is undertaken, shifting focus entirely to spiritual matters.
  • The Saints primarily speak the Holy Tongue (Hebrew) to maintain a sanctified atmosphere.
  • Worldly activities like travel and recreation are avoided.
  • Formal attire, often including white garments and head coverings, is worn during Holy Convocation.
  • Use of electrical appliances is limited to essential needs (e.g., infant heating) to minimize distractions.

The day is marked by communal worship, prayer, study, and personal meditation, culminating in a close-out prayer ceremony that transitions the community back into the regular weekly rhythm. The Sabbath stands as a powerful, recurring testimony to the covenant—a weekly reaffirmation of the community’s dedication to divine rhythms over worldly schedules and priorities.
