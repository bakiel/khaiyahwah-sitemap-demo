Passover (Pessach)

[Image Placeholder: Passover Seder or symbolic imagery of exodus]

Passover (Pessach) holds profound significance for the African Hebrew Israelites, commemorating not only the ancient exodus of Israel from Egyptian bondage but also the modern exodus of the community from spiritual and physical captivity in America.

The observance is deeply connected to the community's departure from America on April 12, 1967, which is viewed as a direct fulfillment of prophecy and the beginning of their liberation journey.

Passover is celebrated with specific ceremonial observances that reflect both historical tradition and the community's unique experiences. These include communal gatherings, specific readings, symbolic foods (prepared according to Genesis Nutrition), and reflections on the themes of liberation, redemption, and divine deliverance.

The dramatic events of the Great Show of Strength in 1986 also occurred during Passover, adding another layer of meaning to this Holy Day as a time when the community's faith and reliance on Yah were tested and vindicated.
