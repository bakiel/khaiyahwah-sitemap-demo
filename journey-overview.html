Our Prophetic Journey: From Captivity to Kingdom

The journey of the African Hebrew Israelites of Jerusalem is a modern-day fulfillment of ancient biblical prophecy. It is a story of divine guidance, spiritual awakening, wilderness purification, and the triumphant return to the Promised Land after centuries of exile. Explore the key milestones of this remarkable exodus:

The Divine Vision (1966)

In Chicago, H.E. Ben Ammi receives a vision from the Angel Gabriel with the divine mandate: gather the scattered children of Israel from America and lead them back to the Holy Land to establish Yah's Kingdom. Prophetic signs confirm the time is near.

The Exodus Begins (1967)

On April 12, 1967, precisely 400 years after the first enslaved Africans arrived in America (1619), the first group of pioneers departs from the land of captivity, embarking on a journey first to Liberia, West Africa.

Wilderness Purification (1967–1969)

The community spends approximately 3.5 years in the Liberian wilderness, facing immense challenges from the elements and limited resources. This period serves as a crucial spiritual refining process, shedding the mentality acquired during captivity.

Return to the Promised Land (1969–1970)

In August 1969, the first pioneers, including Prince Gavriel and Prince Hezkiyahoo, arrive in Israel. They establish initial settlements in the Negev desert towns of Arad and Dimona, marking the physical return to the ancestral homeland.

The Mountain of Life (1971)

The community gathers at Har Kayeem (Mountain of Life) in Dimona. H.E. Ben Ammi establishes the Divine Council, appointing twelve Princes to govern according to divine law, laying the foundation for Kingdom governance and spiritual practices.

The Test of Patience (1973–1986)

The community faces over a decade of intense legal challenges, opposition from authorities, and economic hardship. Through perseverance, faith, and divine protection, they endure this period of testing.

The Great Show of Strength (1986)

During a tense Passover confrontation where authorities attempt to block their pilgrimage, H.E. Ben Ammi leads the community in a five-day fast. This non-violent demonstration of spiritual strength shifts public perception and marks a turning point.
