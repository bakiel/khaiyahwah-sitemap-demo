The Exodus Begins (1967)

[Image Placeholder: Pioneers departing or journey imagery]

Following the divine mandate received by H.E. Ben Ammi, preparations for the exodus commenced swiftly. On April 12, 1967, a date imbued with profound prophetic significance, the first group of pioneers departed from America.

This departure marked the end of precisely 400 years since the arrival of the first enslaved Africans in Jamestown, Virginia, in 1619—fulfilling the timeline of captivity foretold in scripture (Genesis 15:13). It was not merely a migration but a divinely orchestrated exodus from the land of spiritual death and oppression.

The initial group, comprising approximately 400 men, women, and children, embarked on a journey fraught with uncertainty but guided by unwavering faith. Their destination was not immediately Israel, but Liberia, West Africa—a land chosen for a period of wilderness purification before entering the Promised Land.

Led by figures appointed by H.E. Ben Ammi, these pioneers left behind the familiar comforts and constraints of American life, stepping into the unknown based solely on the divine vision and the promise of redemption. This courageous first step initiated the physical return of the Hebrew Israelites after centuries of exile.
