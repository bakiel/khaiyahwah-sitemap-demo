Covenant Remembered (1990)

[Image Placeholder: Community receiving legal recognition or celebrating]

The year 1990 marked a significant milestone in the community's journey, representing the culmination of **twenty years of testing** in the Holy Land. This period aligns with biblical patterns of trial and refinement before divine promises are fulfilled.

Following years of perseverance, diplomatic efforts, and the demonstration of their peaceful and productive presence, the Israeli government granted the community **legal recognition** through B-1 work visas. This decision, documented by Israeli media and government records, provided the first official acknowledgment of their right to live and work in the land.

This recognition was seen by the community as Yah **remembering His covenant** with His people. It validated their faith and endurance through the long "Test of Patience" and confirmed that their presence in the land was divinely ordained.

While full permanent residency and citizenship would come later, the granting of legal status in 1990 was a crucial turning point. It stabilized the community's position, allowed for greater economic participation, and signaled a shift towards integration within Israeli society, fulfilling a key aspect of the prophetic timeline that guided their return.
