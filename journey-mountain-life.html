The Mountain of Life (Har Kayeem) (1971)

[Image Placeholder: Gathering at Har Kayeem or the Divine Council]

On **October 24, 1971**, a pivotal event occurred in the establishment of Yah's Kingdom in the Holy Land. The Saints gathered at a location in Dimona that would become known as **Har Kayeem (Mountain of Life)**. This assembly marked the formal establishment of divine governance for the community.

Following the pattern established by Moses (Exodus 18), H.E. Ben Ammi appointed **twelve Princes** to form the **Divine Council**. Each Prince received a spiritual title reflecting their divine purpose and area of responsibility within the Kingdom (e.g., Prince of Love, Prince of Patience, Prince of Wisdom). H.E. Ben Ammi served as the thirteenth Prince, guiding the council as the spiritual leader.

The establishment of the Divine Council laid the foundation for Kingdom laws, spiritual practices, and community administration. Unlike worldly political systems, this council operated based on principles of righteousness, spiritual insight, and divine law, setting the standard for governance within the burgeoning community. This event solidified the community's structure and provided the leadership framework necessary for navigating the challenges ahead.
