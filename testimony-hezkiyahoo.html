Prince Hezkiyahoo

The Pathfinder

[Image Placeholder: Prince Hezkiyahoo]

Known as "The Pathfinder," Prince Hezkiyahoo was one of the first to arrive in Israel, preparing the way for others to follow. His diplomatic skills helped navigate many early challenges.

"When I arrived in Israel in 1969, I knew we were fulfilling ancient prophecy. Standing on the soil of our ancestors after nearly 400 years in captivity, I felt a connection to something eternal—the unbreakable covenant between Yah and His people."

"In those early days, we faced skepticism and opposition from all sides. Officials questioned our claims to Hebrew identity. Religious authorities challenged our understanding of Scripture. Local residents were wary of these newcomers with strange customs and garments."

"When they said we would not survive three months in Israel, the Holy One of Israel sustained us for twenty years and beyond. What weapon formed against us could prosper when Yah Himself was our shield?"

"I remember the Great Show of Strength in 1986, when authorities surrounded our Village to prevent our Passover pilgrimage to Jerusalem. Rather than respond with force, our Father Ben Ammi called for a five-day fast. The world watched as we demonstrated that our strength comes not from flesh but from spirit. This non-violent response changed the tide of public opinion and opened doors that had previously been closed to us."

"After precisely twenty years—a biblically significant period of testing—the covenant was remembered. In 1990, we received work visas, and later permanent residency. Today, our young people serve in the Israel Defense Forces, our athletes represent Israel in international competitions, and our cultural contributions are recognized throughout the land."

"The lesson is clear: when you align yourself with divine purpose, no earthly power can defeat you. The prophets declared that the children of Israel would return to their homeland in the latter days, and here we stand—living proof of Yah's faithfulness."
